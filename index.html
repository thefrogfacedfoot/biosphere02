~/about.md

hi, welcome in 🌿

this is my corner of the internet — a quiet biosphere sealed in glass, half forest and half star map. drag the windows. plant a seed. draw your own constellation.

nothing here is locked. nothing tracks you. it's just a place.

  • built without frameworks (just html / css / vanilla js)
  • everything is draggable, resizable, minimizable
  • your state survives a refresh (localStorage)
~/devlog/

devlog #10 — jun 29

two more on the pond and one bug fix.

lily pads. a few pads now drift slowly across the water (the count scales with viewport width — usually 4-5). some carry a tiny pink-and-yellow flower. they're drawn on the pond canvas so they share the same shoreline ripples and reflection layer. click one and a small ✦ jumps from the pad with two stacked ripples (big and small from the pad center) — meant to read as a frog blip the moment after a frog leapt. i did not put an actual 🐸 on screen because it'd be too literal; the wake from where it just was is more fun.

twilight owl. at night (or if you've sky-locked to night/dusk in settings) an owl 🦉 will occasionally glide across the upper third of the sky. wing-style vertical bob via a sine on the elapsed-progress, hand-tuned until it stopped feeling like a metronome. click it to spot — counts toward the same creatures-spotted total as the fox. the timer always ticks (~60-130s), but if it's not night when the timer fires, no owl spawns. that way the owl really belongs to night and you can't game it by leaving the tab open.

bug: the "you're viewing a shared sky" banner has been silently showing on every page load since i added it. the html had hidden on it but .shared-banner { display: flex } overrode the browser's default [hidden] { display: none } (same specificity, author wins). added a one-line .shared-banner[hidden] { display: none } right before the existing rule. embarrassing miss, but the kind that only shows up when you're not actually testing the un-shared path.

also discovered the owl emoji is the same flipped, so no need for the facing-left handling i did for the fox. small mercies.

devlog #09 — jun 28, evening

three additions tonight. all of them about giving the bottom half of the screen something to do — i'd been staring at the same fog-and-fireflies layer below the windows for weeks.

the pond. a glassy water surface now sits along the bottom of the screen. it's its own canvas — a dark gradient with a faint moss-tinted shoreline at the top edge and a handful of shimmery reflected stars that drift in place. click anywhere on the water and a ripple expands out (with a warmer inner ring, like a stone broke the surface). every 10-30 seconds an idle ripple drifts in from somewhere on its own, so the water never feels static. respects reduced-motion (idle ripples pause, click ripples still play because they're a response, not ambience).

a fox. every minute or two a small fox 🦊 trots along the shoreline of the pond, left-to-right or right-to-left at random. hover over it and it freezes in place (so you can actually catch the shy thing). click it and a little gold burst fires at the cursor, the count goes up in ~/status, and it slips back into the underbrush. i wanted something that rewarded patience — you can't summon it, you just have to be around when it comes by.

seasons. there's already a day/night cycle. now there's a year cycle too. by default it picks your season from the current month — spring drops faint cherry petals across the page, summer pulses a few extra sparkly fireflies in the upper sky, autumn drifts amber leaves, winter scatters slow snow. the ~/settings window now has a "lock the season" picker next to the sky lock, so if you want it to be perpetually winter you can have that. the body also gets a very subtle whole-page tint per season (warm in autumn, cool in winter, etc).

z-index choreography was the only thing i had to be careful about. the layer order ended up: stars/constellation at the back, then fireflies+fog+pond+season-particles in a middle band, then windows on top, with the fox just above the windows' background plane but below the actual window chrome. the pond captures clicks in its region so the constellation canvas doesn't fire there (which means you can't drop a sky-star into the water, but that felt right — it's water, not sky).

going to sit on the couch and watch the fox pass.

devlog #08 — jun 28, later

two long-overdue things.

the sign. a wooden welcome sign now hangs from the top-center of the desktop, swaying gently. it says the brand and gives you a tiny breadcrumb of how to use the place. you can take it down (× in the corner), and once it's down it stays down. respects reduced-motion (no sway). disappears in stargazer mode. visitors who already explored it once shouldn't have to look at it forever — that felt important.

global search. a real search bar in the topbar now. type anything: it searches window names, mycelium notes (titles AND bodies), every devlog entry, every project, every wish. grouped results dropdown with snippets. arrow keys and enter work. clicking a devlog match jumps to that entry in the ~/devlog/ window, scrolls it into view, and flashes a warm highlight on it for ~2 seconds so you can find what matched. notes pre-fill the editor. windows just focus. wishes pop a toast so you can read them.

i kept the command palette too. they overlap a bit but they're for different jobs: ⌘K is "find a window fast"; the search bar is "find a thing across everything." palette stays narrow and lightning-quick; search is broader and stays in your peripheral vision.

small detail i'm proud of: the snippet rendering escapes html first, then runs a case-insensitive regex over the safe string to wrap matched substrings in <mark>. so if you search for <, the regex still matches because both the snippet text and the query are escaped the same way.

okay. that's the last addition for now.

devlog #07 — jun 28

three more things, all variations on the same theme: rewarding people who actually look up.

stargazer mode. press f (or the new 🌌 button in the topbar) and the whole ui dissolves — windows, topbar, taskbar all fade out and slide off-screen, leaving just the sky and the forest. great for screenshots, great for actually sitting with it for a minute. press anything to come back. i wanted this since the first day but kept putting it off; turned out to be like 15 lines.

catching shooting stars. if you click a shooter while it's still streaking, you catch it — gold burst at your cursor, count goes up in the status window. took me a few tries to get the hit-test right; the trail head is the latest point in the shooter's path, and i ended up with a ~36px catch radius which is generous enough that it feels possible without being trivial. (when you catch your first one you get a small toast.)

lantern. a soft amber glow now follows your cursor when it's over the open sky. it hides over windows and bars (uses document.elementFromPoint to check what's under you each frame, throttled with rAF). a small touch but the whole page reads warmer with it on. respects reduced-motion.

also: the catch click had to take priority over the "drop a new star" click on the same canvas. tried stopImmediatePropagation from a capture-phase listener, didn't trust myself on the event-flow corner cases, ended up just calling tryCatchShooter() at the top of the existing handler. less clever, more obvious. that's usually the right trade.

going outside.

devlog #06 — jun 27, latest

okay, additions day. four new things plus one easter egg.

settings window. i kept testing the reduced-motion fix by toggling macos accessibility, which is awful. so there's a proper settings window now: mute audio, force reduce-motion, lock the sky to a specific mood (dawn / day / dusk / night), and a nuclear "reset everything" button that clears your sky, plant, notes, wishes, settings and window positions in one shot.

mycelium search. embarrassed it didn't have this from day one. type into the search input and the note list filters in real time. empty results show a hint.

share your sky. the constellation studio has a "share my sky" button now. it encodes your stars and lines into a base64 url hash, copies it to your clipboard, and anyone who opens that link sees exactly the sky you drew. when you're viewing someone else's sky there's a banner at the top of the studio with a "return to mine" button. i made sure editing is gated while viewing — no accidentally overwriting their sky with your scribbles, no accidentally saving theirs over yours.

and the easter egg. type the konami code anywhere — ↑↑↓↓←→←→ba — and an overlay opens asking you to make a wish. type something, hit enter, and a slow shooting star drifts across the sky with your wish trailing behind it in warm gold. the wishes save. your status window shows the count. they're private to you.

also discovered while testing: my applyTimeOfDay reassignment didn't work for the periodic 60s tick because setInterval had captured the original function reference at boot. swapped to setInterval(() => applyTimeOfDay(), 60_000) so it does the name lookup each time. classic.

okay. that's enough for one weekend.

devlog #05 — jun 27, later

shipped five things last night feeling pretty good about myself, then woke up this morning and immediately started finding holes.

first one: i'd been testing at one browser size the whole time, and window positions live in localStorage forever. shrunk the browser to write this entry and the contact window was just gone — way off the right edge. now positions get clamped back into view on resize (debounced 160ms so it doesn't fight you while you drag the browser corner).

found out the hard way that prefers-reduced-motion is real and i was ignoring it. flipped the macOS toggle to test (system settings → accessibility → display → reduce motion) and the aurora was still drifting around and the fireflies were still bouncing. felt bad. added a media query that kills the animation rules and hides the fireflies entirely. shooting stars stop spawning too.

ambient orbit had a bug i'd never have caught without leaving it running for a while: started the drone, switched tabs to do something else, came back a minute later — silence. chrome suspends the AudioContext when the tab is hidden and i wasn't resuming it. one visibilitychange listener and it works.

little stuff while i was in there:

  • mycelium textarea had no max-height so you could drag it past the window bottom. clamped to 140px.
  • plant water button accepted clicks at any rate, which would queue up hundreds of particle elements if you spammed. throttled to ~4 hz.
  • found opacity: 1.2 on the dusk aurora. browsers silently clamp that to 1, so the rule never did anything. replaced with a filter: saturate brightness combo that actually does the warm sunset thing.

no new features today. just sanding the edges.

devlog #04 — jun 27

okay it's late and i couldn't sleep so i opened the laptop. i kept thinking the site was pretty but inert — you'd drag the windows around once and then there was nothing waiting for you. so today was about adding things that respond to being here over time.

ambient orbit exists now. i was scared of the web audio api for no reason. two detuned saw waves through a low-pass filter that opens and closes slowly, plus a little bell that pings on a random pentatonic note about once a minute. it's the kind of sound you don't notice until you turn it off. exactly what i wanted.

mycelium notes — this one i'm proudest of. you write notes, you link them with [[name]] in the body, and hovering one highlights every other note it touches. it's not novel, it's just roam/obsidian in 80 lines of vanilla js, but it feels almost magical that it works at all.

other stuff while i was in there:

  • shooting stars. one streaks every 15-30s. they were too straight at first so i added a faint arc.
  • the background actually shifts based on what time it is for you. dawn gets a pink wash, dusk goes orange, deep night is the navy you've been seeing. i was up at 5am writing this and got to test the dawn state for real.
  • command palette. press ⌘K or just /. honestly added it because i kept losing windows behind other windows.

going to bed.
